A Yankee in the Sunshine State

I spent 60 years gardening up North. Then Florida humbled me.

Hi, I'm Maggie. After a lifetime of New York winters and a career I finally retired from, my husband and I packed up for the Gulf Coast. I thought I knew how to garden. Turns out I had to learn it all over again, and I'm sharing every muddy lesson here.

Hello from the porch

From a fifth-floor walk-up to a backyard full of sunshine

For most of my life, gardening meant a few pots of geraniums on a Brooklyn fire escape, a community plot I fought tooth and nail for, and a deep, abiding love of spring bulbs that survived the long gray months. I knew frost dates by heart. I knew when the last hard freeze usually let go and how to harden off seedlings on a chilly windowsill.

Then we retired, sold the apartment, and moved to a little house near the Florida Gulf. Suddenly my forty years of hard-won Northern know-how were about as useful as a snow shovel in July. The seasons flipped. The soil turned to sand. The bugs got bigger and bolder. This blog is where I write down what I'm learning, mistakes and all, so the next transplanted Yankee has an easier go of it.

What you'll find here

Honest gardening for the rest of us

I am not a master gardener with a certificate on the wall. I am a curious retiree with dirt under her nails, a stack of seed catalogs, and forty years of Northern habits I am slowly rewiring. Yankee Gardener is a place for real talk about what works, what flopped, and what I would do differently. Expect plant profiles tested in my own beds, seasonal to-do lists that finally make sense for the Gulf Coast, and the occasional story about the iguana who keeps eating my hibiscus. If you have just moved south and your thumb suddenly feels a lot less green, pull up a rocking chair. You are exactly who I am writing for.

Topics I cover

A little of everything that keeps a Florida garden growing

Whether you have just unpacked the moving boxes or you have wintered here for years, there is something in the journal for you.

Adjusting from up North

The mindset shifts that turned my frustrating first year around, from flipped seasons to the end of winter dormancy. If your trusty Northern habits suddenly stopped working, start here. I cover the planting calendar that finally made sense for the Gulf Coast and the instincts you can safely leave behind in the snow.

Plants that love the heat

Honest profiles of the shrubs, flowers, and herbs that thrive when summer turns brutal. Firebush, plumbago, bougainvillea, crotons, native wildflowers, and the heat-proof herbs I keep by the kitchen door. Every plant here has been tested in my own sandy beds, so you get the real verdict, not the catalog version.
